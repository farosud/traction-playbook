# Targeting Blogs — Traction Channel 11

Get your product in front of audiences that already exist by contributing to the publications they trust. "Blogs" in 2026 means Substacks, newsletters, niche media, YouTube channels, podcasts, and actual blogs.

## Service Layer
- Exa.ai: discover blogs/newsletters covering your space by semantic search
- Firecrawl: scrape blog content to analyze format, tone, submission guidelines
- Hunter.io: find editor/author email addresses
- Apollo.io: enrichment on blog owners/editors
- Serper: find blogs ranking for your target keywords (they have the audience you want)
- Jina Reader: quick-read any blog post for format analysis (free fallback)

## On Invocation

### 1. Gather Context

```
PROJECT:          [name]
ONE-LINER:        [what it does]
TARGET AUDIENCE:  [who are you trying to reach]
EXPERTISE AREAS:  [what can you credibly write about?]
EXISTING CONTENT: [do you have a blog, newsletter, past guest posts?]
ASSETS:           [data, case studies, unique insights you can offer?]
```

### 2. Blog/Newsletter Discovery

Find 20-30 publications your audience reads. Three methods:

#### Method A: Audience-First Search (Exa.ai)
```
QUERIES:
  "best [topic] newsletter"
  "best blog for [audience type]"
  "[persona job title] reads"
  "[your category] insights blog"
  "weekly [industry] newsletter"
  "substack [your niche]"

For each result, capture:
  NAME:              [publication name]
  TYPE:              [blog / Substack / newsletter / YouTube / podcast]
  AUTHOR:            [who writes/runs it]
  AUDIENCE SIZE:     [subscribers/followers/traffic estimate]
  AUDIENCE OVERLAP:  [how much does their audience match your target? 1-10]
  POSTING FREQUENCY: [daily / weekly / monthly]
  ACCEPTS GUESTS:    [yes / no / unclear]
  RECENT RELEVANT:   [title of a recent post related to your space]
  CONTACT:           [email / DM / submission form URL]
```

#### Method B: Keyword-Reverse Search (Serper)
```
Search your target keywords on Google.
The blogs ranking on page 1-2 HAVE the audience you want.

Search: "[your problem keyword] guide"
Search: "[your solution keyword] review"
Search: "best [your category] tools"
Search: "[your audience] tips"

Extract: which blogs keep appearing? Those are your targets.
```

#### Method C: Competitor Backlink Mining
```
Search for articles ABOUT your competitors or adjacent products.
The blogs that wrote about them will write about you.

Search: "[competitor name] review"
Search: "[competitor name] alternative"
Search: "[competitor name] integration"

These writers already cover your space — they need new content.
```

### 3. Score & Prioritize

For each publication:

```
PUBLICATION: [name]

AUDIENCE OVERLAP (0-3):
  3 = their audience IS your target persona
  2 = significant overlap
  1 = some overlap
  0 = wrong audience

ACCEPTANCE LIKELIHOOD (0-3):
  3 = actively seeking guest posts / open submissions
  2 = occasionally features external contributors
  1 = mostly staff-written but occasionally accepts
  0 = closed / very selective

TRAFFIC/REACH (0-2):
  2 = large audience (10K+ subscribers or 50K+ monthly visits)
  1 = moderate audience (1K-10K)
  0 = tiny audience (<1K)

SEO VALUE (0-2):
  2 = high domain authority, dofollow links
  1 = moderate DA
  0 = low DA or nofollow only

TOTAL: ___/10

PRIORITY:
  8-10 = Tier 1 (pitch this week)
  5-7  = Tier 2 (pitch this month)
  1-4  = Tier 3 (park for later)
```

### 4. Angle Generation

For each Tier 1 publication, generate a TAILORED pitch angle. This is the critical step — don't pitch the same thing to everyone.

```
PUBLICATION: [name]
THEIR RECENT CONTENT: [3 titles from their last month]
THEIR AUDIENCE CARES ABOUT: [topics they consistently cover]

ANGLE OPTIONS:
  1. [Extend their narrative]
     They wrote about [X]. Your angle: "Here's what happens AFTER [X]"
     or "The part about [X] nobody talks about"

  2. [Provide data they don't have]
     "We analyzed [N] [things] and found [surprising insight]"
     Original data/research is the easiest way to get published anywhere.

  3. [Counter-narrative to trending topic]
     They (or their competitors) said [popular opinion].
     You say: "Actually, here's why [contrarian take] is true, with evidence."

  4. [Case study/story format]
     "How [product/approach] helped [specific person] achieve [result]"
     Stories > opinion pieces for acceptance rate.

  5. [Framework/tool their audience needs]
     "The [N]-step framework for [problem their audience has]"
     Practical, actionable content gets accepted most often.

RECOMMENDED ANGLE: [which one + why it fits THIS publication]
```

### 5. Pitch Crafting

```
PITCH EMAIL TEMPLATE:

Subject: [Guest post] [specific catchy title] for [publication name]

Hi [name],

I've been reading [publication] for [time period] — your piece on [specific recent article] particularly resonated because [genuine reason].

I'd love to contribute a piece on [TITLE]:
[2-3 bullet points on what the piece covers]

Why this fits [publication name]:
- [Specific reason tied to their editorial direction]
- [What unique value you bring — data, experience, case study]
- [How their audience benefits]

I've written for [previous publications if any, or "here's a sample of my writing: [link]"].

Happy to send a draft or adjust the angle. What do you think?

[Your name]
[One-line bio + link]
```

RULES:
- Subject line must include the word "guest post" or "contribution" — editors filter for this
- Reference a SPECIFIC recent article of theirs (proves you actually read the publication)
- Lead with THEIR audience's benefit, not your product pitch
- Include bullet points of what the piece covers — editors scan, they don't read carefully
- Keep total pitch under 150 words (editors get 50+ pitches/day)
- NEVER attach the full draft unsolicited — offer to send it

### 6. Article Writing Framework

Once accepted, write the piece following this structure:

```
ARTICLE STRUCTURE:

HOOK (first 2 sentences):
  Start with a surprising fact, question, or story.
  NOT "In this article I will discuss..."

PROBLEM (paragraph 1-2):
  Describe the problem the reader has.
  Use language THEY use (pull from Reddit/Twitter).

FRAMEWORK/SOLUTION (bulk of article):
  Your unique perspective, data, or framework.
  This should be genuinely valuable standalone.
  Don't hold back — the more value, the more shares.

PRODUCT MENTION (1-2 sentences, naturally):
  "When we built [product], we found that..."
  or "Tools like [product] approach this by..."
  NEVER a hard sell. The value of the article IS the marketing.

TAKEAWAY (final paragraph):
  Actionable next step the reader can do TODAY.
  Optional: link to a resource (your site, but make it genuinely useful).

AUTHOR BIO (at the bottom):
  [Name] is the founder of [product] ([one-liner]).
  [Link to product] | [Link to Twitter/X]
```

RULES:
- 1,200-2,000 words for blog posts (sweet spot for shareability + SEO)
- Include at least one original image, chart, or framework visual
- Link to 2-3 of THEIR previous articles (editors love this — internal links help their SEO)
- Product mention: MAX 2 times in the entire article. If forced, zero is better.
- Write for THEIR audience, not yours. Match their publication's tone and depth.

### 7. Distribution After Publication

Getting published is step 1. Amplifying it is step 2.

```
DAY OF PUBLICATION:
  - Share on your Twitter/X with a hook (not just "check out my guest post")
  - Share on LinkedIn with a professional angle
  - Post in relevant subreddits (summarize, link, add value in comments)
  - Email to your list: "I wrote about [topic] in [publication]"
  - Tag the publication + editor on social (they'll reshare)

WEEK AFTER:
  - Repurpose key points into a Twitter thread
  - Create a LinkedIn carousel from the frameworks
  - Cross-post a summary to your own blog (with canonical link back)
  - Submit to Hacker News if technical enough

ONGOING:
  - Monitor comments on the original post — respond to all
  - Track traffic from the post (UTM parameters or referral data)
  - Note which publications drove actual signups vs just traffic
```

### 8. The Newsletter Angle

Newsletters are often easier than blogs and sometimes more effective:

```
NEWSLETTER STRATEGY:

  Tier 1: Get featured AS content
    - Pitch the newsletter owner a guest edition or featured section
    - Provide unique data/insight they can share with credit
    - "I have data on [topic] that your readers would love. Want me to write up the key findings?"

  Tier 2: Get mentioned in their recommendations
    - Many newsletters have a "tools I'm using" or "links of the week" section
    - Just ask: "Would [product] be relevant for your [section name]?"
    - Provide a one-liner they can copy-paste

  Tier 3: Sponsor the newsletter
    - Many niche newsletters accept sponsors for $50-500 per issue
    - Write the ad copy yourself — make it match their tone
    - Track: unique link → signups → conversion rate
    - If CPA < your threshold, scale up
```

### 9. The Podcast Angle

Podcast appearances are a form of "targeting blogs" with audio:

```
PODCAST DISCOVERY:
  - Search ListenNotes for "[your topic] podcast"
  - Look for shows with 100-5K downloads per episode (reachable hosts)
  - Shows that interview founders/builders (not just news shows)

PODCAST PITCH:
  Subject: Guest idea — [specific catchy topic]

  "Hi [host name],

  Love what you're doing with [show name]. Your episode with [recent guest] on [topic] was great.

  I'd love to come on and talk about [specific topic — NOT your product pitch]:
  - [Interesting angle 1]
  - [Interesting angle 2]
  - [What their audience would learn]

  Quick context: I'm [name], founder of [product]. [One sentence credibility.]

  Here's my calendar if you're interested: [link]

  [name]"

RULES:
  - Pitch the TOPIC, not yourself
  - The host needs to imagine their audience being interested
  - Offer 2-3 topic options (gives them choice, increases acceptance)
  - Have a "podcast bio" and headshot ready to send
```

### 11. Channel Fit Assessment

Blog targeting is likely strong for you if:
- Your product solves a problem people write/read about
- You can write well or have unique data/insights to share
- Your target audience reads niche publications (not just mainstream news)
- You have credibility in your space (even a little — your own product counts)
- You're willing to invest 4-8 hours per guest post

Blog targeting is likely NOT your channel if:
- Your product is too early to explain clearly
- There are no publications covering your niche
- You can't write and don't have data/stories to share
- Your audience doesn't read blogs/newsletters (some audiences don't)

Timeline:
- Week 1: Discovery + scoring (20-30 publications identified)
- Week 2-3: First batch of pitches sent (5-10)
- Week 4-6: First publications go live
- Month 2-3: Rhythm established, 2-4 guest posts per month
- Month 6: Regular contributor relationships, steady referral traffic
