# Business Development (BD) — Traction Channel 12

Partnership-driven growth. Not networking for networking's sake — structured identification and pursuit of relationships where both sides gain customers, credibility, or capability.

## Service Layer
- Exa.ai: ecosystem discovery — find complementary tools, communities, newsletters, platforms in your space
- Apollo.io: contact finding — identify decision-makers at target partner companies
- Hunter.io: email verification and discovery for BD contacts
- Firecrawl: company research — scrape partner sites for integration pages, partner directories, tech stack info

## On Invocation

### 1. Context Gathering

Before running any BD strategy, gather:

- What does your product do? (one sentence)
- Who is your target user? (role, company size, industry)
- What tools does your user already use alongside yours? (the existing stack)
- What communities does your user belong to? (Slack, Discord, forums, associations)
- Do you have an API or integration capability? (even basic webhooks count)
- What's your current user count and growth rate?
- Have you done any partnerships before? What happened?
- What's your capacity to support partnerships? (dedicated person, or founder doing it?)
- What would a dream partnership look like for you?

### 2. Ecosystem Mapping

Build a map of everything that surrounds your product. Four quadrants:

#### Quadrant A: Complementary Tools
Products your users also use. Not competitors — complements.
```
TOOL NAME | WHAT IT DOES | OVERLAP WITH YOUR USERS | INTEGRATION POTENTIAL
---------|-------------|------------------------|---------------------
[tool]   | [function]  | [high/med/low]         | [API/embed/data/none]
```

Use Exa.ai queries:
- "tools that integrate with [competitor or adjacent tool]"
- "[your category] tech stack" or "[your audience] toolkit"
- "best tools for [your user's job-to-be-done]"

Generate 15-25 complementary tools.

#### Quadrant B: Communities & Platforms
Where your users already gather.
```
COMMUNITY | TYPE | SIZE | ACTIVITY LEVEL | PARTNERSHIP ANGLE
----------|------|------|---------------|------------------
[name]    | [Slack/Discord/forum/association] | [members] | [high/med/low] | [sponsor/content/integration]
```

Generate 10-15 communities.

#### Quadrant C: Content & Education Partners
People/orgs teaching your audience.
```
PARTNER | TYPE | AUDIENCE SIZE | CONTENT FIT | PARTNERSHIP ANGLE
--------|------|--------------|------------|------------------
[name]  | [course creator/newsletter/podcast/YouTuber] | [reach] | [high/med/low] | [co-create/sponsor/feature]
```

Generate 10-15 education partners.

#### Quadrant D: Distribution Channels
Marketplaces, directories, platforms where your product could be listed or embedded.
```
CHANNEL | TYPE | TRAFFIC/USERS | LISTING EFFORT | POTENTIAL IMPACT
--------|------|--------------|----------------|------------------
[name]  | [marketplace/directory/platform] | [estimate] | [easy/moderate/hard] | [high/med/low]
```

Generate 5-10 distribution channels.

### 3. Partner Scoring Framework

Score each potential partner on three dimensions. Multiply for composite score.

```
PARTNER: [name]

AUDIENCE OVERLAP (1-10):
  - Do they serve the same persona? [yes=8-10, adjacent=4-7, different=1-3]
  - Scoring notes:

EASE OF INTEGRATION (1-10):
  - Technical: Can you actually build/ship this? [API exists=8-10, possible=4-7, hard=1-3]
  - Relationship: Can you reach someone? [warm intro=8-10, cold but findable=4-7, fortress=1-3]
  - Average of technical + relationship scores
  - Scoring notes:

MUTUAL VALUE (1-10):
  - What do THEY get from this? [significant value=8-10, moderate=4-7, mostly you benefit=1-3]
  - Scoring notes:

COMPOSITE SCORE: [overlap] x [ease] x [mutual value] = ___/1000

PRIORITY TIER:
  500+  = Tier 1 (pursue immediately)
  200-499 = Tier 2 (pursue this quarter)
  <200  = Tier 3 (park for later)
```

Score your top 20 partners from the ecosystem map. Rank by composite score.

### 4. Partnership Types & Structures

#### Type A: Tool Integration Partnership
Best for: complementary SaaS tools
Structure:
- Build a two-way integration (data flows both directions if possible)
- Co-list on each other's integration pages
- Co-announce via email/social to both audiences
- Optional: revenue share on referred signups

Proposal framework:
```
INTEGRATION CONCEPT: [what connects]
YOUR SIDE: [what you build/provide]
THEIR SIDE: [what they build/provide]
USER BENEFIT: [why end users care]
TIMELINE: [realistic build estimate]
CO-MARKETING PLAN: [announcement, listing, ongoing promotion]
```

#### Type B: Community Partnership
Best for: Slack groups, Discord servers, professional associations
Structure:
- Provide genuine value to the community (workshops, templates, tools)
- NOT just sponsoring a logo placement
- Offer free/discounted access for community members
- Co-create content specific to that community

Proposal framework:
```
COMMUNITY: [name]
VALUE YOU PROVIDE: [workshop/template/free tier/AMA/tool]
ASK: [promotion to members, featured partner, channel access]
EXCLUSIVITY: [are you willing to be exclusive in your category?]
COST: [sponsorship fee if any, or pure value exchange]
```

#### Type C: Content & Education Partnership
Best for: course creators, newsletter writers, podcasters, YouTubers
Structure:
- Co-create content (not just "sponsor my thing")
- Case studies using both products together
- Guest teaching in their course/community
- Provide data/research they can feature

Proposal framework:
```
PARTNER: [name]
CO-CREATION IDEA: [specific content concept]
YOUR CONTRIBUTION: [data/expertise/access/tool]
THEIR CONTRIBUTION: [audience/platform/credibility]
FORMAT: [blog/video/podcast/workshop/case study]
DISTRIBUTION: [who publishes where]
```

#### Type D: Distribution/Marketplace Partnership
Best for: app stores, directories, platform ecosystems
Structure:
- Build to their spec (Shopify app, Zapier integration, etc.)
- Optimize your listing for their discovery algorithm
- Get early reviews/ratings
- Participate in their partner programs

Proposal framework:
```
PLATFORM: [name]
LISTING TYPE: [app/integration/plugin/template]
BUILD EFFORT: [hours/weeks estimate]
DISCOVERY POTENTIAL: [how users find you on their platform]
REVENUE MODEL: [free/paid/freemium on their marketplace]
```

### 5. BD Outreach Sequence

#### Pre-Outreach Research (per partner)
Use Firecrawl + Exa.ai to gather:
- Their current integration partners (who have they already partnered with?)
- Their partnership page / BD contact info
- Recent announcements, launches, funding
- Mutual connections (check LinkedIn via Apollo)
- Their content strategy (what do they publish about?)

#### Cold Outreach Sequence

**Email 1 — The Value-First Opener (Day 1)**
```
Subject: [specific observation about their product] + [your product]

Hi [name],

I've been watching [their company] — [specific genuine compliment about something they shipped/wrote/launched recently].

I run [your product], which [one sentence what you do]. Our users frequently also use [their product] for [specific use case], and I keep hearing requests for [specific integration/connection point].

I sketched out what a [integration/partnership type] could look like:
- [Benefit 1 for their users]
- [Benefit 2 for their users]
- [What you'd handle on your side]

Would you be open to a 20-minute call to explore whether this makes sense?

[your name]
```

**Email 2 — The Evidence Follow-Up (Day 4)**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Quick follow-up — I dug into this more and found:
- [X] of our users also use [their product] (pulled from our data)
- [Specific user quote or request mentioning their product]
- [Link to a mockup/doc showing what the integration could look like]

Happy to share the full analysis if helpful.

[your name]
```

**Email 3 — The Mutual Connection or Social Proof (Day 8)**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

[Name], one more thought:

[Option A: We both know [mutual connection] — happy to have them intro us properly.]
[Option B: We just launched a similar partnership with [other partner] and it drove [specific result]. Happy to share what we learned.]

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Just don't want to leave a good idea on the table.

[your name]
```

**Email 4 — The Clean Break (Day 14)**
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

Closing the loop — if partnership isn't a priority right now, totally understand. I'll keep building and circle back when the timing is better.

If you want to revisit later: [link to your partnership page or calendar]

[your name]
```

### 6. Partnership Meeting Framework

When you land the meeting, structure it:

**First 5 min: Rapport + Context**
- How did they start their company?
- What are they focused on this quarter?

**Next 10 min: Mutual Discovery**
- What does their user journey look like?
- Where do users drop off or ask for more?
- What integrations/partnerships have worked best for them?
- What hasn't worked?

**Next 10 min: Your Proposal**
- Present the specific partnership concept
- Show mockup/doc if you have one
- Emphasize what THEY get (lead with their benefit)

**Last 5 min: Next Steps**
- Who else needs to be involved on their side?
- What's a realistic timeline?
- Define a small pilot vs. full partnership

### 7. Integration Architecture (Technical Partnerships)

If the partnership involves a technical integration, define:

```
INTEGRATION SPEC:
  Type: [API-to-API / webhook / embed / OAuth / data sync]
  Data Flow: [one-way or bidirectional]
  Your endpoint: [what you expose]
  Their endpoint: [what you consume]
  Auth: [API key / OAuth2 / webhook secret]
  Rate limits: [what's acceptable]
  Error handling: [retry policy, failure notifications]

BUILD PLAN:
  Phase 1 (MVP): [minimal viable integration, 1-2 weeks]
  Phase 2 (Full): [complete feature set, timeline]
  Phase 3 (Polish): [docs, onboarding flow, marketplace listing]

MAINTENANCE:
  Who monitors uptime?
  Breaking change notification process?
  Support escalation path?
```

### 8. First Move — Quick-Start Templates

Pick one based on your situation:

**If you have an API but no partners yet:**
1. List 5 complementary tools from Quadrant A
2. Build a one-way integration with the easiest one (even unofficial)
3. Tweet/post about it and tag them
4. Use their reaction as the opener for a formal partnership convo

**If you have no API but want community partnerships:**
1. List 3 communities from Quadrant B
2. Create a genuinely useful resource for that community (template, guide, tool)
3. Share it in the community with no pitch attached
4. After positive reception, approach the community leader about a formal partnership

**If you want distribution partnerships:**
1. Pick the marketplace/directory with the lowest barrier to entry
2. Build and submit your listing this week
3. Ask 5 existing users to leave reviews
4. Use this as proof of concept for bigger platforms

- Meeting-to-proposal rate (target: 50%+)

- Time from first outreach to live partnership (benchmark: 4-8 weeks)

### 10. Channel Fit Assessment

BD is likely a strong channel for you if:
- [x] Your users already use complementary tools (ecosystem exists)
- [x] You have or can build integration capability
- [x] Partners would gain clear value (not just you extracting their audience)
- [x] You can dedicate 5-10 hours/week to BD relationships
- [x] Your product is stable enough that a partner won't regret associating with you
- [x] You're targeting B2B or prosumer markets (BD is weaker for pure consumer)

BD is likely NOT your channel if:
- [ ] You're pre-product-market-fit (partners need you to be reliable)
- [ ] Your product is in a category of one with no adjacent tools
- [ ] You can't commit to maintaining integrations long-term
- [ ] Your founding team has zero industry connections to start from

BD timeline expectations:
- Week 1-2: Ecosystem mapping + partner scoring complete
- Week 3-4: First outreach batch sent (10-15 partners)
- Week 5-8: First meetings, first partnership in negotiation
- Month 3: 2-3 partnerships live, measuring initial results
- Month 6: 5-10 active partnerships, BD contributing measurable growth
