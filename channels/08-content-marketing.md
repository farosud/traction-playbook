# Content Marketing — Traction Channel 8

## Service Layer
- Exa.ai: find what content your audience is already consuming, discover gaps
- Firecrawl: scrape competitor content to analyze what works
- Serper: keyword research, check what ranks for target topics
- Jina Reader: read any URL for content analysis (free fallback)
- Replicate/FAL.ai: generate visual assets for content
- Resend: distribute content via newsletter

## On Invocation

### 1. Gather Context
```
PROJECT:          [name]
ONE-LINER:        [what it does]
TARGET AUDIENCE:  [who reads your stuff]
FOUNDER STRENGTHS: [writer / speaker / technical / visual]
EXISTING CONTENT: [any blog, newsletter, social accounts?]
CONTENT GOAL:     [awareness / leads / SEO / thought leadership]
FREQUENCY:        [how often can you realistically publish?]
```

### 2. Define Content Pillars

Generate 3-5 content pillars. These are TOPICS, not formats.

```
BAD PILLARS (too vague):
  - "AI news"
  - "Startup tips"
  - "Product updates"

GOOD PILLARS (specific to audience pain):
  - "Decision frameworks for solo founders"
  - "What my AI council said about [specific decision]"
  - "Real founder decisions: before vs after"
  - "The mistakes nobody talks about"
```

Each pillar should map to a specific persona pain signal from the lead gen research.

RULES:
- Never write about your PRODUCT. Write about your audience's PROBLEMS.
- The product appears naturally as "here's how I/we/someone solved this"
- 80% value, 20% product mention — and that 20% should feel organic

### 3. Content Calendar

Generate a 30-day calendar:

```
WEEK 1:
  Mon: [pillar] — [specific title] — [format: thread/post/video/newsletter]
  Wed: [pillar] — [specific title] — [format]
  Fri: [pillar] — [specific title] — [format]

WEEK 2: ...
WEEK 3: ...
WEEK 4: ...
```

Rules for the calendar:
- Mix formats: don't do 30 blog posts. Alternate threads, posts, videos, newsletters
- Front-load the best ideas (attention span is highest at launch)
- One "anchor" piece per week (long, deep, shareable) + 2 "satellite" pieces (shorter, distribute the anchor's ideas)
- Include repurposing: every anchor piece should become 3-5 satellite pieces

### 4. The Anchor-Satellite Model

```
ANCHOR PIECE (1x/week):
  Long-form. 1,500-3,000 words or 10+ minute video.
  Deep value. This is what gets bookmarked and shared.
  Targets a specific keyword for SEO.

  BECOMES →

SATELLITE PIECES (3-5 per anchor):
  - Twitter/X thread (key points from anchor)
  - LinkedIn post (one insight, professional angle)
  - Reddit comment (answer a question using anchor's framework)
  - Newsletter section (summarize for email subscribers)
  - YouTube Short / TikTok (one key takeaway, 60 seconds)
  - Instagram carousel (visual version of the framework)
```

### 5. Distribution Strategy

Content without distribution is a diary. For each piece:

```
PLATFORM        FORMAT              TIMING              TONE
──────────────  ──────────────────  ──────────────────  ──────────────
Twitter/X       Thread or hook      Morning (8-10 AM)   Casual, punchy
LinkedIn        Professional post   Tue-Thu (9 AM)      Insights-focused
Reddit          Genuine comment     When relevant posts  Helpful, no shill
                                    appear (monitor)
Hacker News     Show HN or comment  When relevant        Technical, humble
Newsletter      Weekly digest       Same day each week   Personal, deep
YouTube         Video or Short      Thu-Sat              Educational
Indie Hackers   Building in public  Milestone moments    Transparent
```

KEY INSIGHT: Don't just "post and pray." Actively participate in conversations where your content is relevant. The distribution IS the engagement.

### 6. Content Hooks That Work

For the titles/hooks, use these patterns:

```
PROVEN HOOKS:
  "I [did X] and here's what happened"              → story-driven
  "The [thing] nobody talks about in [industry]"    → contrarian
  "How to [achieve result] without [common pain]"   → solution
  "I spent [time] researching [topic]. Here's..."   → authority
  "Stop [common bad advice]. Do this instead."      → provocative
  "[Number] [things] I wish I knew about [topic]"   → listicle
  "Why [counterintuitive claim] is actually true"   → contrarian
  "The [decision] that [result] my [business]"      → case study

BAD HOOKS:
  "Check out our new feature!"                      → nobody cares
  "Introducing [product name]"                      → nobody cares
  "We're excited to announce..."                    → nobody cares
```

### 7. The Meta-Content Play

If the product generates interesting outputs (like an AI council debating), the product itself IS content:

```
PRODUCT-AS-CONTENT:
  - Record council sessions → edit into content
  - Screenshot interesting advisor debates → tweet them
  - "My AI [advisor type] said [surprising thing]" → thread
  - User-generated: ask permission to share anonymized sessions
  - "What happens when you ask an AI council about [trending topic]"

This creates a flywheel:
  Product creates content → Content attracts users →
  Users create more content → More content attracts more users
```

### 8. Competitive Content Analysis

Before writing, analyze what's already ranking/performing:

```
USING EXA (if available):
  Search for: "[topic] [audience]"
  Find: top 10 pieces of content on this topic
  Analyze: what angle did they take? what's missing?
  Strategy: write the piece they SHOULD have written

USING SERPER (if available):
  Search target keywords
  Analyze top 10 SERP results
  Find: content gaps, outdated info, missing depth
  Strategy: 10x the best existing piece

MANUAL:
  Check Reddit/Twitter for questions about the topic
  Look for "someone should write about [X]" signals
  Find the questions nobody's answering well
```

### 9. Measurement

```
LEADING INDICATORS (check weekly):
  - Views/impressions per piece
  - Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares, saves)
  - Click-through to site/product
  - New email subscribers per piece
  - Which pillar drives the most engagement?

LAGGING INDICATORS (check monthly):
  - Organic search traffic
  - Domain authority growth
  - Direct signups attributable to content
  - Newsletter subscriber growth rate
  - Content-influenced revenue

MEMORY TRACKING:
  After each batch, record in memory:
  - Which topics resonated (engagement > average)
  - Which formats performed best
  - Which platforms drove the most traffic
  - Which hooks got clicks vs which flopped
  → Next month's calendar is informed by this data
```

### 10. Quick Win Content Ideas

Generate 5 pieces that can be published THIS WEEK with minimal effort:

```
FORMAT:
  TITLE:    [specific, hook-driven title]
  PILLAR:   [which content pillar]
  FORMAT:   [thread / post / article / video]
  PLATFORM: [where to publish first]
  EFFORT:   [30 min / 1 hr / 2 hrs / half-day]
  HOOK:     [the first line that makes people stop scrolling]
  REPURPOSE: [what else this becomes]
```

These should be "low effort, high signal" — pieces that demonstrate expertise and product value without requiring deep research or production.
