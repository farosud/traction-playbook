# Unconventional PR — Traction Channel 3

## Service Layer
- Exa.ai: research successful stunts in similar spaces
- Replicate/FAL.ai: generate creative assets for stunts
- Firecrawl: analyze competitor PR stunts
- SociaVault: track social amplification of the stunt

## On Invocation

### 1. Gather Context
- Product/startup + what it does
- Brand personality (serious, playful, edgy, wholesome, weird)
- Risk tolerance (conservative, moderate, bold, YOLO)
- Budget for stunts ($0-50, $50-500, $500-5K, $5K+)
- Founder's comfort with public attention (introvert vs extrovert)

### 2. Stunt Ideation
Generate 5-8 stunt/campaign concepts across these categories:

CATEGORY A — DAVID VS GOLIATH
Challenge a bigger player in a creative way. Not hostile — playful.
Example template: "We spent $[tiny amount] on [thing] vs [competitor] spending $[huge amount] on [same thing]. Here's what happened."

CATEGORY B — SOCIAL EXPERIMENT
Do something that generates genuine curiosity/discussion.
Example template: "We gave [product] to [interesting group of people] for [time period] and [surprising result]."

CATEGORY C — RADICAL TRANSPARENCY
Share something companies normally hide.
Example template: "Here's our exact [revenue/costs/mistakes/strategy]. Everything."

CATEGORY D — PHYSICAL-DIGITAL CROSSOVER
Do something in the physical world that goes viral online.
Example template: "We [physical action] and the internet [reaction]."
(The /billboard skill is a sub-case of this.)

CATEGORY E — CHALLENGE / CONTEST
Create a competition that involves your audience.
Example template: "We challenged [audience] to [task] using [product]. Winner gets [prize]."

CATEGORY F — META / SELF-REFERENTIAL
Make the marketing itself the product demo.
Example template: "This [ad/post/stunt] was created by [product feature]."

FORMAT per stunt:
  STUNT NAME:
  CATEGORY: [A-F]
  CONCEPT: [2-3 sentence description]
  BUDGET: [estimated cost]
  VIRALITY POTENTIAL: [1-10]
  RISK LEVEL: [low/medium/high]
  EXECUTION STEPS: [numbered list]
  AMPLIFICATION PLAN: [how to ensure people see it]
  CONTENT GENERATED: [what social content comes FROM the stunt]
  PRESS ANGLE: [why a journalist would cover this]
  THE "SANE VERSION": [80% of impact for 20% of risk]

### 3. Risk Assessment
For each stunt:
- What could go wrong?
- Could this backfire or offend?
- Is it on-brand or desperate?
- Would the founder be embarrassed in 5 years?
- Legal considerations?

### 4. Amplification Plan
The stunt itself is 30% of the value. The other 70% is:
- Tweet thread documenting the stunt
- Reddit post (choose the right sub)
- LinkedIn post (different angle for professional audience)
- YouTube short or TikTok of the moment
- Blog post: "We did [stunt]. Here's what we learned."
- Send to journalists who covered similar stunts before

### 5. Measurement
- Social impressions/engagement
- Press mentions generated
- Website traffic spikes
- New user signups attributable to the stunt
- Content pieces generated from the stunt
- Cost per impression vs paid alternatives
