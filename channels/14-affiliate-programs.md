# Affiliate Programs — Traction Channel 14

Pay other people a commission for sending you customers. Aligned incentives — they promote, they earn, you grow.

## Service Layer
- Exa.ai: discover potential affiliates (newsletter writers, YouTubers, bloggers in your space)
- Apollo.io: find contact info for potential affiliate partners
- Hunter.io: email discovery and verification
- Firecrawl: scrape potential affiliate sites to assess audience and fit
- Resend: send affiliate recruitment emails and program updates
- Serper: find people who write "best [category] tools" or "[competitor] review" articles

### 2. Program Structure Design

Choose a program type and design the economics:

#### Option A: Simple Referral (User-to-User)
```
STRUCTURE:
  Referrer gets:  [free month / credit / cash / feature unlock]
  Referred gets:  [discount / free trial extension / bonus]

EXAMPLES:
  Dropbox: both get extra storage
  Notion: both get $10 credit
  Robinhood: both get a free stock

BEST FOR: consumer/prosumer products, viral-loop strategy
ECONOMICS: low cost per referral, high volume potential
```

#### Option B: Revenue Share (Affiliate Partners)
```
STRUCTURE:
  Affiliate gets: [X%] of referred customer's [first payment / recurring / lifetime]

  COMMON STRUCTURES:
    20-30% of first month  → simple, low risk for you
    20-30% recurring       → strong incentive, higher lifetime payout
    50% of first month     → aggressive, attracts more affiliates
    One-time bounty ($X)   → predictable cost, easier to budget

BEST FOR: B2B tools, SaaS with clear monthly pricing
ECONOMICS: higher cost per acquisition but only pay for results
```

#### Option C: Tiered Program
```
STRUCTURE:
  Bronze (0-10 referrals/mo):    20% commission
  Silver (11-50 referrals/mo):   25% commission
  Gold (50+ referrals/mo):       30% commission + dedicated support

  Higher tiers also get:
  - Early access to new features
  - Co-marketing opportunities
  - Custom landing pages
  - Direct Slack/Discord access to founders

BEST FOR: products with both casual and professional promoters
```

#### Commission Calculator
```
YOUR ECONOMICS:
  Price:                $[X]/mo
  Gross margin:         [X]%
  Customer LTV:         $[X] (avg months × price)
  Max affordable CPA:   $[X] (LTV × acceptable payback ratio)

  Commission at 20%:    $[X] per signup per month
  Commission at 30%:    $[X] per signup per month

  Breakeven: affiliate must refer [N] customers for the program
  to be worth the admin overhead.

  TARGET: total affiliate commission should be < 30% of LTV
```

### 3. Affiliate Discovery

Find people who SHOULD be promoting you:

#### Type A: Content Creators in Your Niche
```
SEARCH QUERIES (Exa/Serper):
  "best [your category] tools [year]"
  "[your category] review"
  "[competitor name] vs"
  "my [audience type] tech stack"
  "tools I use for [job your product does]"

TARGET: people who ALREADY write about tools like yours
WHY: they have the audience AND the context to promote naturally
```

#### Type B: Newsletter Writers
```
SEARCH QUERIES:
  "newsletter for [your audience]"
  "[your niche] weekly digest"
  "substack [your topic]"

TARGET: newsletters with 1K-50K subscribers in your niche
WHY: one mention in the right newsletter = dozens of signups
BONUS: many newsletters NEED sponsors — you're offering them revenue
```

#### Type C: YouTube/Podcast Creators
```
SEARCH:
  YouTube: "[your category] tutorial"
  YouTube: "best tools for [audience]"
  ListenNotes: podcasts about [your topic]

TARGET: creators with 1K-100K audience (reachable, engaged)
WHY: video/audio reviews build deep trust — highest conversion rate
```

#### Type D: Existing Happy Users
```
FIND:
  Users who tweet/post about your product
  Users with high engagement/NPS scores
  Users who have large audiences themselves
  Users who've already referred friends organically

TARGET: users who love the product AND have reach
WHY: authentic advocacy > paid promotion every time
```

#### Type E: "Tools" Bloggers / Comparison Sites
```
SEARCH:
  "[your product] vs [competitor]" — who wrote this?
  "best [category] tools" — who ranks for this?
  "[audience] toolkit" roundup articles

TARGET: sites that rank for comparison/review keywords
WHY: people reading these articles are ACTIVELY shopping — highest intent
```

### 4. Affiliate Scoring

```
AFFILIATE CANDIDATE: [name/site]

AUDIENCE MATCH (0-3):
  3 = their audience IS your target buyer
  2 = significant overlap
  1 = some overlap
  0 = wrong audience

REACH (0-3):
  3 = 10K+ engaged followers/subscribers
  2 = 1K-10K
  1 = 100-1K
  0 = <100

CONTENT QUALITY (0-2):
  2 = produces high-quality, trusted content
  1 = decent content
  0 = low quality or spammy

PROMOTION STYLE (0-2):
  2 = authentic, integrates products naturally into content
  1 = standard affiliate approach
  0 = spammy, aggressive, could damage your brand

TOTAL: ___/10

PRIORITY:
  8-10 = Recruit immediately, offer premium terms
  5-7  = Recruit this month
  3-4  = Add to list, recruit when program is proven
  0-2  = Skip
```

### 6. Affiliate Marketing Materials

Create a "swipe file" affiliates can use:

```
AFFILIATE KIT CONTENTS:

1. PRODUCT ONE-LINER:
   "[Product] is [what it does] for [who it's for]."

2. VALUE PROPOSITIONS (pick any):
   - "[Product] helps [audience] [achieve result] without [pain point]"
   - "I switched to [product] because [specific differentiator]"
   - "[Product] saved me [time/money] by [how]"

3. SOCIAL MEDIA COPY (ready to post):
   Twitter/X: "[Hook]. [Brief explanation]. Try it: [link]"
   LinkedIn: "[Professional angle on the value prop]. [link]"
   Instagram: "[Casual/lifestyle angle]. Link in bio."

4. EMAIL COPY (for newsletter sponsors):
   Short version (50 words)
   Medium version (100 words)
   Long version (200 words)

5. VISUAL ASSETS:
   - Product screenshots (key features)
   - Logo in multiple formats
   - Banner ads (728x90, 300x250, 160x600)
   - Social media images (1200x628 for Twitter, 1080x1080 for Instagram)

6. COMPARISON CHART:
   [Product] vs [Competitor A] vs [Competitor B]
   Feature-by-feature comparison that makes you look good (honestly)

7. FAQ FOR AFFILIATES:
   - How do I track my referrals?
   - When do I get paid?
   - What's the cookie duration?
   - Can I run ads with my affiliate link?
   - Can I offer a custom discount?
```

### 9. Program Optimization

```
MONTHLY REVIEW:

WHAT'S WORKING:
  - Which affiliates drive the highest-quality customers? (not just volume)
  - Which channels convert best? (newsletter > blog > social?)
  - Which messaging/angles perform best?

ACTION:
  - Give top performers: higher commission, exclusive access, co-marketing
  - Share top-performing copy with underperformers
  - Remove affiliates that drive zero results after 60 days
  - Recruit 5-10 new affiliates per month

QUALITY CONTROL:
  - Monitor how affiliates represent your product
  - Check that they're not using misleading claims
  - Ensure they disclose the affiliate relationship (FTC compliance)
  - Watch for self-referral fraud (signing up through own link)
```

### 10. The Meta-Play: Product-Integrated Referrals

```
BUILD REFERRAL INTO THE PRODUCT ITSELF:

- Share buttons on results/outputs the user is proud of
  (e.g., "Share this insight from your AI council")

- "Powered by [product]" watermark on free-tier outputs
  (every output is a mini-ad)

- Milestone prompts: "You've used [product] 50 times!
  Know someone who'd love this? [share link]"

- Team/collaboration features that require inviting others
  (product spreads because using it involves sharing)

The best affiliate program doesn't feel like an affiliate program.
It feels like sharing something you love.
```

### 11. Channel Fit Assessment

Affiliate programs are likely strong for you if:
- You have a product people genuinely love (they'd recommend it anyway)
- Your pricing supports giving away 20-30% commission
- Content creators exist in your niche who review/recommend tools
- Your product is easy to explain in a tweet or paragraph
- You can provide tracking links and a basic dashboard

Affiliate programs are likely NOT your channel if:
- Your product is too early / changing too fast (affiliates need stability)
- Your niche has no content creators or review culture
- Your margins can't support commissions
- Your product requires a complex sales process (hard for affiliates to sell)
- You're in a heavily regulated industry where affiliate claims are risky

Timeline:
- Week 1: Program structure designed, materials created
- Week 2-3: First 10-20 affiliates recruited
- Month 1: First referrals coming in, measuring conversion rates
- Month 2: Optimize commission structure, double down on top affiliates
- Month 3-6: Steady affiliate channel contributing 10-25% of new customers
